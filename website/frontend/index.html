Listening…

How Sanjeevani gets from your voice to an answer

  1. 1

    Listen

    Your speech is transcribed in its original language using AI4Bharat's IndicConformer model, trained on the IndicVoices corpus.

  2. 2

    Translate

    The transcript is translated into English with AI4Bharat's IndicTrans2, so nothing is lost in how the question was asked.

  3. 3

    Answer

    Google's Gemma 4 (served via Ollama) drafts a cautious, plain-language response — grounded in a WHO fact sheet when your question matches one, and remembering earlier turns in the same conversation.

What this build is — and isn't, yet

Sanjeevani is a prototype. The listening and translation are powered by real, pretrained AI4Bharat models. When your question matches one of a small, curated set of WHO fact sheets, the answer is grounded in a live excerpt from who.int and says so, with a source link. Outside that set, the answer is drafted by Gemma 4 with extra caution and no specific source — that's why those answers keep the "draft" stamp. Growing the WHO topic coverage (or connecting a different verified clinical source) is the natural next step, not a hidden one.

Built on

  • AI4Bharat IndicConformer — speech recognition
  • AI4Bharat IndicTrans2 — translation, both directions
  • AI4Bharat Indic Parler-TTS — spoken answers
  • Google Gemma 4, served via Ollama Cloud — answer drafting
  • World Health Organization fact sheets — grounding, where matched

Sanjeevani is a health-information prototype. It does not diagnose and is not a substitute for professional medical care. In an emergency, call 108.
